18  In the middle of February 2008, I became unsatisfied with the current homebrew framework I was using for
19  the Emeraldion Lodge, EmePavilion. I felt the need for a solid platform that was object-oriented, fully
20  <acronym title="Model View Controller">MVC</acronym> compliant, and had an
21  <acronym title="Object Relational Mapping">ORM</acronym> layer that relieved me from having to hardcode
22  repetitive query patterns.
23  </p>
24  <p>
25  I then tried to write from scratch a lightweight clone of
26  <a href="http://www.rubyonrails.org" class="external">Ruby on Rails</a>,
27  by replicating much of their ActiveRecord model class, and a convention-based development model.
28  I struggled against the syntactical and practical limitations of the language I am bound to with my hosting,
29  <a href="http://www.php.net" class="external">PHP</a>, but at the end I had a working framework in less
30  than a month.
31  </p>
32  <p>
33  EmeRails supports page caching, action filtering and a lot of useful features that save coding time and
34  server load.
35  I am quite satisfied about the result, and I am looking forward to improving it and smudging the edges
36  that are still rough in the future.
