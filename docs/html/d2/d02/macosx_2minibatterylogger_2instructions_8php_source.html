1 <h2>Instructions</h2>
2 <p>
3  When you launch MiniBatteryLogger, it starts monitoring your laptop battery.
4  The default view is the chart of the charge and amperage over time. Anyway,
5  you will have to wait some time to see interesting stuff ;)
6 </p>
7 <p class="centered">
8  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/chartview.png"
9  alt="The Chart View" />
10 </p>
11 <p>
12  The chart view shows a graph of the charge of the battery and its amperage
13  versus time. Additionally,
14  a bubble icon will mark each power event: a change of power source, the
15  beginning and end of charge, sleep and wakeup.
16 </p>
17 <p class="centered">
18  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/sessionbrowser.png"
19  alt="The Sessions Browser" />
20 </p>
21 <p>
22  Monitoring sessions can be saved to disk for later analysis &mdash; by default, automatically.
23  To manage past sessions, use the Sessions Browser, provided as a drawer.
24  You can visualize a session just clicking on it, while the application will
25  continue monitoring the battery in the background.
26 </p>
27 <p>
28  The Battery Inspector shows you all the available details about your battery:
29  charge, amperage, voltage, capacity &mdash; current and maximum &mdash; time
30  to empty when on battery power and time to full when charging; number of full
31  cycles.
32 </p>
33 <p class="centered">
34  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/inspector.png"
35  alt="The Battery Inspector" />
36 </p>
37 <p>
38  By default, the application changes its Dock icon to reflect the current
39  conditions of the battery.
40  You can also show a battery window on the Desktop if you want to see the
41  status of the battery
42 </p>
43 <p>
44  Both icons change their color according to the current amount of charge,
45  just like <a href="/software/widgets/minibatterystatus/">MiniBatteryStatus widget</a>.
46 </p>
47 <p class="centered">
48  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/desktop_battery_icon.png"
49  alt="Desktop battery window" />
50 </p>

52 <h3>Statistics</h3>
53 <p class="centered">
54  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/statsview.png"
55  alt="The Statistics View" />
56 </p>
57 <p>
58  MiniBatteryLogger keeps a record of your battery health stats. Every time
59  the cycle count increases or the maximum capacity decreases, MiniBatteryLogger
60  saves a <strong>&ldquo;snapshot&rdquo;</strong> of your battery, with date and an optional
61  user comment &mdash; you can also create and delete snapshots manually, of course.
62 </p>
63 <p>
64  Snapshots let you evaluate the evolution of your battery performance along
65  time, to detect malfunctioning or estimate the remaining battery life.
66 </p>
67 
68 <h3>Comparison</h3>
69 <p class="centered">
70  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/compview.png"
71  alt="The Comparison View" />
72 </p>
73 <p>
74  By sharing your battery data, you are contributing to the Shared Battery Data
75  Archive, a project to collect and organize Mac laptop battery data.
76 </p>
77 <p>
78  The Archive makes the <strong>Comparison</strong> feature possible.
79  Have you ever wondered if your battery is &ldquo;good&rdquo; or compared
80  to other batteries?
81  Now with MiniBatteryLogger&rsquo;s comparison tools you can compare your
82  battery with all other batteries of the same kind in the Archive.
83 </p>
84 <p>
85  The Shared Battery Data Archive is also accessible through a nice
86  <a href="http://burgos.emeraldion.it/mbl/">web interface</a>.
87 </p>

89 <h3>The Battery Log</h3>
90 <p class="centered">
91  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/logview.png"
92  alt="The Log View" />
93 </p>
94 <p>
95  MiniBatteryLogger logs to disk all relevant battery and power source events.
96  The Log View shows the current log from the start of the current session.
97  Every time an event is caught, a line is appended to the log. A search field
98  lets you filter the lines of interest (e.g. when you&rsquo;re just searching
99  for the power chord disconnections, type &ldquo;battery power&rdquo;).
100 </p>

103 <h3>Preferences</h3>
104 <p>
105  Every user has different needs. MiniBatteryLogger lets you customize about
106  every parameter involved in monitoring the battery and report of data.
107 </p>
108 <p class="centered">
109  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/preferences-general.png"
110  alt="General Preferences" />
111 </p>
112 <p>
113  The <strong>General</strong> pane lets you choose the time interval of active
114  battery polling and to enable or disable the visualization of the battery status
115  in the application&rsquo;s Dock icon.
116 </p>
117 <p class="centered">
118  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/preferences-chartview.png"
119  alt="Chart View Preferences" />
120 </p>
121 <p>
122  The <strong>Chart View</strong> pane lets you choose the colors to be used in
123  the graph of charge over time.
124 </p>
125 
126 <p class="centered">
127  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/preferences-notifications.png"
128  alt="Notifications Preferences" />
129 </p>
130 <p>
131  From the <strong>Alerts</strong> pane you can customize the way MiniBatteryLogger
132  warns you about critical events. You can choose the level of charge under which the
133  application should issue a Low Battery notification, and the level of capacity
134  under which a Battery Near Death warning should be issued.
135 </p>
136 <p class="centered">
137  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/growl_alert.png"
138  alt="Low battery Growl alert" />
139 </p>
140 <p>
141  You can choose to be notified either with Growl alerts
142  or speech synthesized vocal alerts.
143 </p>
144 
145 <p class="centered">
146  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/preferences-swupdate.png"
147  alt="Software Update Preferences" />
148 </p>
149 <p>
150  The <strong>Software Update</strong> pane lets you enable the automatic update check
151  at startup. You can also see when the last update check has been performed.
152  An additional button lets you immediately check if there is an available update.
153 </p>
154 
155 <p class="centered">
156  <img src="/assets/images/software/macosx/minibatterylogger/preferences-sharing.png"
157  alt="Sharing Preferences" />
158 </p>
159 <p>
160  Finally, the <strong>Sharing</strong> pane lets you configure the way
161  MiniBatteryLogger will interact with the world. From the <strong>Shared Battery Data
162  Archive settings</strong> box you can set up MiniBatteryLogger to automatically send
163  your own data to the Archive and retrieve shared data for your computer model.
164 </p>
165 <p>
166  Battery data is sent to the Archive <em>anonymously</em>. No sensible information
167  will be ever sent without your consent.
168 </p>
169 <p>
170  From the <strong>Data Sharing settings</strong> box you can also
171  set up MiniBatteryLogger
172  to look for shared batteries and to share your own data over the network.
173 </p>
174 <p>
175  You may also decide not to advertise the service with Bonjour or to require a
176  password to access your data (currently not implemented).
177 </p>
